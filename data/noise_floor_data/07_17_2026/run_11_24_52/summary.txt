====================================================================
 NOISE FLOOR  —  the noise each photodiode adds on its own
====================================================================
Extra electronic/pickup noise per channel, measured only where no
carrier or interference lives, then scaled to the analysis band.
Record : 150000 samples (30 s)   Fs = 5000 Hz   carrier = 930.5 Hz
Scaled : to the 4 Hz demod window (same band the cross-sensor cell uses)

  sensor     noise(uV)    Var(n) mV^2  vs quietest   consistency
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  BPW34_1         28.2       7.97e-04        1.0x    consistent
  BPW34_2         54.3       2.95e-03        3.7x    consistent
  Inano_1         88.2       7.77e-03        9.8x       varies*
  Inano_2         50.6       2.56e-03        3.2x    consistent

How to read this
  noise(uV)    the channel's own noise, in microvolts, over the demod band.
  Var(n) mV^2  the same thing as a variance — it IS the Var(n_i) the
               cross-sensor cell prints for this diode.
  vs quietest  how many times noisier than the best channel.
  consistency  'consistent' = same floor everywhere; 'varies*' = the floor
               changes with frequency (usually a 1/f rise down low), so read
               the number as a typical mid-band value, not an exact constant.

Where each number came from
  BPW34_1   averaged over 74 clean bands (e.g. 195, 1744, 1094 Hz);  32 rejected as too near a spur
  BPW34_2   averaged over 100 clean bands (e.g. 1744, 27, 609 Hz);  6 rejected as too near a spur
  Inano_1   averaged over 105 clean bands (e.g. 1956, 195, 206 Hz);  1 rejected as too near a spur
  Inano_2   averaged over 94 clean bands (e.g. 1755, 2264, 42 Hz);  12 rejected as too near a spur

Rejected bands are ones whose floor changed with window width — spur
leakage (the carrier and the 1 kHz MCU line), not real diode noise.
